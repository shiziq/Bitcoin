Preamble

Engagement Whereas

The Client operates a technology consulting and product development business including but not limited to blockchain-based payment systems, fintech applications, and digital infrastructure. The Client desires to engage the Consultant for its current and future technical projects.

WHEREAS:

(A) Batolink Global Limited (the "Client") is a Kenyan technology company specialising in blockchain infrastructure, stablecoin payment systems, fintech product development, digital wallet solutions, point-of-sale payment technologies, NFC wearable devices, and related digital financial services.

(B) The Client's current flagship project is the Bato USDT Stablecoin Payment System (the "Bato Project") — an end-to-end digital payment infrastructure integrated into the KCB Bank mobile application, comprising a USDT digital wallet, POS merchant payment system with QR code protocol, in-app authenticator with message-based confirmation, optional NFC wearable bracelet (PayBand), multi-chain blockchain infrastructure spanning Polygon POS and Arbitrum, and a dual-currency (USDT + KES) display engine with live rate aggregation.

(C) The Client may from time to time engage in additional projects, products, and services in the technology, fintech, and blockchain sectors (together with the Bato Project, the "Projects").

(D) The Consultant possesses specialised expertise in software architecture, product requirements documentation, UI/UX design systems, blockchain development, customer journey mapping, and technical project management.

(E) The Client desires to engage the Consultant to provide product strategy, technical documentation, design systems, and project development services for the Projects, and the Consultant is willing to provide such services on the terms and conditions set forth herein.

NOW, THEREFORE, the Parties hereby agree as follows:

1. Engagement and Services

1.1 Engagement

The Client hereby engages the Consultant, and the Consultant hereby accepts the engagement, to provide and perform the services described in Schedule 1 of this Agreement (the "Services") for the Projects.

1.2 Standard of Services

All Services shall be performed with promptness, diligence, and proficiency expected of a consultant with the background and expertise the Consultant has represented. The Client shall provide such access to its information, personnel, and resources as may be reasonably required to permit the Consultant to perform the Services.

1.3 Key Personnel

The Services specified in Schedule 1 shall be performed by the Consultant personally and may not be assigned or subcontracted without the Client's prior written consent. The Consultant warrants that they are competent and possess the necessary skills, qualifications, and experience to perform the Services to the reasonable satisfaction of the Client.

Section 2

Obligations of the Consultant

The Consultant shall perform all duties with professionalism, integrity, and in the best interests of the Client.

2. Obligations of the Consultant

The Consultant shall:

(i) Act as a consultant to the Client for all Projects undertaken during the term of this Agreement;

(ii) Use their best endeavours to achieve the scope of work outlined in Schedule 1 of this Agreement;

(iii) Act honestly, in good faith, and not do anything harmful to the Client's interests;

(iv) Act jointly with one or more persons as the Client may from time to time direct;

(v) Do all things in the ordinary course of business which the Client reasonably considers necessary or proper in its interests;

(vi) Devote reasonable time and attention to the Client's Projects as agreed between the Parties;

(vii) Deal in an efficient and prompt manner with all requests emanating from the Client or its duly authorised representatives;

(viii) Both during and after the term of this Agreement, keep confidential and not divulge, communicate to any person, or make use of, and use best endeavours to prevent the publication or disclosure of all Confidential Information;

(ix) Do all things reasonably required by the Client to give effect to this Agreement;

(x) Not act in any way that may result in the Client incurring criminal or civil liability;

(xi) Promptly disclose to the Client any information that comes into the Consultant's knowledge or possession which adversely affects the Client;

(xii) Do all things reasonably required by the Client to perfect or protect the intellectual property rights of the Client.

Section 3

Appointment & Term

The engagement shall be for an initial term with provisions for renewal and extension by mutual agreement.

3. Appointment and Term

3.1 Initial Term

The Client appoints the Consultant for a term of ____________ with effect from ____________ until ____________.

3.2 Renewal

Upon expiry of the Initial Term, this Agreement shall automatically renew for successive periods of three (3) months each unless either Party gives the other written notice of non-renewal at least thirty (30) days prior to the expiry of the then-current term.

3.3 Extension by Agreement

The Parties may, by mutual written agreement, extend the term of this Agreement for any duration beyond the Initial Term or any Renewal Term.

3.4 Independent Consultant Status

The Consultant is and shall at all times remain an independent consultant. Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed as creating a partnership, joint venture, employer-employee relationship, or principal-agent relationship. The Consultant shall not be entitled to any pension, bonus, leave allowance, or other fringe benefit, and shall be solely responsible for all income tax liabilities and other statutory contributions.

Section 4

Consultant Fee

The Client shall pay the Consultant a fixed fee net of withholding tax, with additional performance-based compensation.

4.1 Fixed Fee

In consideration of the Services, the Client shall pay the Consultant a fixed fee of Kenya Shillings Two Hundred and Ten Thousand, Five Hundred and Twenty-Six and Thirty-Two Cents (KES 210,526.32) (the "Gross Fee").

4.2 Withholding Tax

The Gross Fee is inclusive of withholding tax at five percent (5%). The Client shall deduct KES 10,526.32 and remit to the KRA. The Consultant shall receive a net payment of Kenya Shillings Two Hundred Thousand (KES 200,000.00).

Gross Fee (before tax)KES 210,526.32
Withholding Tax (5%)− KES 10,526.32
Net Fee payable to ConsultantKES 200,000.00
4.4 Payment Terms

The Consultant shall deliver invoices upon completion of deliverables. The Client shall pay such invoices within seven (7) days of receipt.

4.5 Performance-Based Compensation

In addition to the Fixed Fee, the Consultant shall be entitled to additional compensation: Option A — a one-time lump sum agreed upon completion; Option B — an agreed percentage of gross earnings derived by the Client from Projects in which the Consultant has been directly involved. The Parties shall discuss and agree upon the applicable option(s) in writing.

Section 5

Intellectual Property

All work product and deliverables created under this Agreement shall be the exclusive property of the Client.

5. Intellectual Property

5.1 Ownership

All work product, deliverables, designs, code, documentation, architecture, user interfaces, brand assets, inventions, and any other materials created by the Consultant in the course of performing the Services (collectively, the "Work Product") shall be the sole and exclusive intellectual property of Batolink Global Limited. The Consultant hereby irrevocably assigns, transfers, and conveys to the Client all right, title, and interest in and to the Work Product, including all copyrights, patents, trade secrets, trademarks, and other intellectual property rights, and all moral rights to the fullest extent permitted by law.

5.2 Work-for-Hire

The Work Product shall be deemed "work made for hire" as defined under the Copyright Act of Kenya (Cap 130, Laws of Kenya).

5.3 Assistance

The Consultant shall, at the Client's request and expense, execute all documents and take all actions reasonably necessary to assist the Client in obtaining, perfecting, maintaining, and enforcing intellectual property protection for the Work Product worldwide.

Sections 6–10

Confidentiality · Termination · General Provisions

6. Confidentiality

The Consultant is bound by the Non-Disclosure Agreement (Ref: NDA-BATO-2026-001) executed between Batolink Global Limited and Nextplay Solutions Limited, which the Consultant has reviewed and agreed to be bound by as if they were a party thereto.

7. Termination

7.1

This Agreement may be terminated at any time by either Party giving thirty (30) days' written notice.

7.2

Either Party may terminate with immediate effect for material breach, where the breach is not remedied within fifteen (15) days of written notice.

7.3

Upon termination, the Client shall pay the Consultant for all Services satisfactorily rendered prior to termination.

8. Expenses

Out-of-pocket expenses must be supported by receipts and pre-approved in writing by the Client.

9. Force Majeure

Neither Party shall be liable for failure or delay due to events beyond their reasonable control.

10. Indemnification

The Consultant shall indemnify and hold harmless the Client from any claims arising out of the Consultant's breach or negligence in performing the Services.

Sections 11–15

General Provisions

11. Non-Solicitation

For twelve (12) months following termination, neither Party shall solicit any employee, contractor, or consultant of the other Party involved in the Projects.

12. Dispute Resolution

Disputes shall first be referred to good-faith negotiation. If unresolved within thirty (30) days, the dispute shall be referred to binding arbitration in accordance with the Arbitration Act (Cap 49, Laws of Kenya), in Nairobi.

13. Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the Republic of Kenya. Each Party submits to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of Kenya, sitting in Nairobi.

14. General Provisions

14.1 Entire Agreement

This Agreement, together with the NDA and any Schedules, constitutes the entire understanding between the Parties.

14.2 Modification

No amendment shall be valid unless in writing and signed by both Parties.

14.3 Severability

If any provision is held invalid, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force.

14.4 No Waiver

No failure or delay in exercising any right shall constitute a waiver.

14.5 Counterparts

This Agreement may be executed in counterparts. Electronic signatures shall have the same force as original signatures.

15. Schedule 1 — Scope of Work and Deliverables

The Consultant shall provide the following Services for the Bato Project and any additional Projects:

  • Product Requirements Documentation: Comprehensive PRDs including technical specifications, architecture diagrams, database schemas, API specs, and smart contract interfaces.
  • Customer Journey Mapping: End-to-end journey maps, screen flow architectures, and user experience documentation.
  • UI/UX Design Systems: Mockups, mobile screen designs, interactive prototypes, and design system documentation.
  • Executive Presentation Materials: Presentation decks, slideshows, and visual communication materials.
  • Technical Architecture: System architecture design including multi-chain blockchain infrastructure.
  • Brand and Visual Identity: Brand assets, logos, colour systems, typography, and visual identity guidelines.
  • Hardware Product Design: Documentation for the PayBand NFC wearable bracelet.
  • Competitive Analysis: Market analysis and strategic recommendations.
  • Any other deliverables reasonably requested and mutually agreed upon in writing.
