Mocked Blockchain — How It Works

The prototype simulates blockchain transactions locally. When Jane loads USDT, the app shows the full screen flow — including the authenticator confirmation dialog — and displays a fake transaction hash. No actual Polygon or Arbitrum contracts are called. The user experiences the complete journey as if it were live. Real blockchain integration is scoped for Phase 2.

Justification

Why This Price is Fair

1. Documentation Already Complete

The PRD v5, Customer Journey Maps, UI/UX designs, screen flows, and brand assets are already created. The developer needs zero time for discovery, wireframing, or design — saving ~50 hours of traditional development effort.

2. Mocked Blockchain — Reduced Complexity

No Polygon or Arbitrum smart contracts deployed. No backend rate engine or auth service. The prototype simulates blockchain TXs locally — full frontend experience without backend infrastructure. Saves ~35 hours.

3. Fixed Fee — Zero Budget Risk

KES 50,000 is the total. The Client will not be charged for scope changes, additional hours, or unforeseen complexity. Project risk sits with the Consultant, not the Client.

4. Play Store Ready — Immediate Stakeholder Access

Published on Google Play, accessible via link. Investors, bank executives, regulators, and partners download and experience the product on their own phones. More compelling than slides or screen recordings.

5. Source Code Delivered — Full Ownership

The Client receives the complete Flutter source code and owns it outright. Continue development with any developer — no vendor lock-in. All intellectual property belongs to Batolink Global Limited.

6. Prototype = Fundraising & Partner Readiness

A working Play Store prototype validates the product to investors, enables partnership discussions with KCB, and demonstrates regulatory readiness to the Central Bank of Kenya. It transforms the Bato project from a document into a tangible product.
