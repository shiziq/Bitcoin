Why This Matters

Rivaling M-Pesa · Flat Fee vs Percentage Fee

M-Pesa is the undisputed king of Kenyan mobile money — but it charges a percentage-based fee per transaction. Send KSh 10,000 and you pay KSh 100–300. Bato flips the economics entirely: every USDT transfer costs approximately KSh 0.15 on Polygon — a flat blockchain fee, not a percentage. That's a 99.9% cost reduction for the average Kenyan user.

Executive Takeaway: A Kenyan business processing KSh 500,000 in monthly payments through M-Pesa pays approximately KSh 5,000–15,000 in fees. The same volume on Bato costs approximately KSh 500 in blockchain fees plus the bank's 2% spread on loads — a fraction of M-Pesa's percentage model. For high-volume merchants, this is a commercially transformative cost structure. And every shilling saved is a shilling that stays in the Kenyan economy.
