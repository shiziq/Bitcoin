Preamble

Engagement
Whereas

The Client operates a technology consulting and product development business including but not limited to blockchain-based payment systems, fintech applications, and digital infrastructure. The Client desires to engage the Consultant for its current and future technical projects.

2

WHEREAS:

(A) Batolink Global Limited (the "Client") is a Kenyan technology company specialising in blockchain infrastructure, stablecoin payment systems, fintech product development, digital wallet solutions, point-of-sale payment technologies, NFC wearable devices, and related digital financial services.

(B) The Client's current flagship project is the Bato USDT Stablecoin Payment System (the "Bato Project") — an end-to-end digital payment infrastructure integrated into the KCB Bank mobile application, comprising a USDT digital wallet, POS merchant payment system with QR code protocol, in-app authenticator with message-based confirmation, optional NFC wearable bracelet (PayBand), multi-chain blockchain infrastructure spanning Polygon POS and Arbitrum, and a dual-currency (USDT + KES) display engine with live rate aggregation.

(C) The Client may from time to time engage in additional projects, products, and services in the technology, fintech, and blockchain sectors (together with the Bato Project, the "Projects").

(D) The Consultant possesses specialised expertise in software architecture, product requirements documentation, UI/UX design systems, blockchain development, customer journey mapping, and technical project management.

(E) The Client desires to engage the Consultant to provide product strategy, technical documentation, design systems, and project development services for the Projects, and the Consultant is willing to provide such services on the terms and conditions set forth herein.

NOW, THEREFORE, the Parties hereby agree as follows:

1. Engagement and Services

1.1 Engagement

The Client hereby engages the Consultant, and the Consultant hereby accepts the engagement, to provide and perform the services described in Schedule 1 of this Agreement (the "Services") for the Projects.

1.2 Standard of Services

All Services shall be performed with promptness, diligence, and proficiency expected of a consultant with the background and expertise the Consultant has represented. The Client shall provide such access to its information, personnel, and resources as may be reasonably required to permit the Consultant to perform the Services.

1.3 Key Personnel

The Services specified in Schedule 1 shall be performed by the Consultant personally and may not be assigned or subcontracted without the Client's prior written consent. The Consultant warrants that they are competent and possess the necessary skills, qualifications, and experience to perform the Services to the reasonable satisfaction of the Client.

Section 4

Consultant
Fee

The Client shall pay the Consultant a fixed fee net of withholding tax, with additional performance-based compensation.

8

4. Consultant Fee

4.1 Fixed Fee

In consideration of the Services to be rendered as outlined in Schedule 1, the Client shall pay the Consultant a fixed fee of Kenya Shillings Two Hundred and Ten Thousand, Five Hundred and Twenty-Six and Thirty-Two Cents (KES 210,526.32) (the "Gross Fee").

4.2 Withholding Tax

The Gross Fee is inclusive of withholding tax at the prevailing statutory rate of five percent (5%) applicable to consultancy services under the Income Tax Act (Cap 470, Laws of Kenya). The Client shall deduct withholding tax of KES 10,526.32 from the Gross Fee and remit the same to the Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA). The Consultant shall receive a net payment of Kenya Shillings Two Hundred Thousand (KES 200,000.00) (the "Net Fee").

4.3 Fee Breakdown

Gross Fee (before tax)KES 210,526.32
Withholding Tax (5%)− KES 10,526.32
Net Fee payable to ConsultantKES 200,000.00

The Client shall provide the Consultant with a withholding tax certificate upon remittance of the tax to the KRA.

4.4 Payment Terms

The Consultant shall deliver invoices to the Client upon satisfactory completion of the deliverables set forth in Schedule 1, supported by a summary of work completed. The Client shall pay such invoices within seven (7) days of receipt and approval.

4.5 Performance-Based Compensation

In addition to the Fixed Fee, the Consultant shall be entitled to additional compensation as follows, to be agreed upon between the Parties in writing:

  • Option A — Lump Sum: A one-time lump sum payment to be mutually agreed upon by the Parties upon successful completion and delivery of all Services under this Agreement, such amount to reflect the scope, complexity, and commercial value of the Services rendered.
  • Option B — Revenue Participation: An agreed percentage of the gross earnings, revenue, or commercial value derived by the Client from the Projects in which the Consultant has been directly involved, such percentage and calculation methodology to be agreed upon by the Parties in a separate written addendum to this Agreement.

The Client and the Consultant shall discuss and agree upon which option (or combination thereof) shall apply, and the specific terms thereof, prior to the submission of the final invoice under this Agreement. Such agreement shall be recorded in writing and appended to this Agreement as an addendum.

7. Termination

7.1 Termination by Notice

This Agreement may be terminated at any time by either Party giving the other thirty (30) days' written notice.

7.2 Termination for Breach

Either Party may terminate this Agreement with immediate effect by written notice if the other Party is in material breach of any term of this Agreement and, where the breach is capable of remedy, fails to remedy such breach within fifteen (15) days of receiving written notice specifying the breach.

7.3 Effect of Termination

Upon termination, the Client shall pay the Consultant for all Services satisfactorily rendered and deliverables delivered prior to the date of termination. The Consultant shall promptly return or destroy all Confidential Information and property belonging to the Client.

The Consultant may include in an invoice a claim for out-of-pocket expenses reasonably incurred in the proper provision of the Services, provided that such expenses are supported by receipts or vouchers and have been pre-approved in writing by the Client.

9. Force Majeure

Neither Party shall be liable for any failure or delay in performance due to an event of force majeure including but not limited to acts of God, civil commotion, government restrictions, extreme weather, pandemics, riots, war, sabotage, and terrorism. The affected Party shall promptly notify the other and use reasonable efforts to minimise the effects.

10. Indemnification

The Consultant shall indemnify and hold harmless the Client from and against any claims, damages, losses, liabilities, costs, and expenses (including reasonable legal fees) arising out of the Consultant's breach of this Agreement or any negligent act or omission in the performance of the Services.

11. Non-Solicitation

For a period of twelve (12) months following the termination of this Agreement, neither Party shall solicit or induce any employee, contractor, or consultant of the other Party who was involved in the Projects to terminate their relationship with that Party.

12. Dispute Resolution

Any dispute arising out of this Agreement shall first be referred to good-faith negotiation between the Parties. If unresolved within thirty (30) days, the dispute shall be referred to binding arbitration in accordance with the Arbitration Act (Cap 49, Laws of Kenya), with the arbitration to take place in Nairobi.

13. Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the Republic of Kenya. Each Party irrevocably submits to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of Kenya, sitting in Nairobi.

15. Schedule 1 — Scope of Work and Deliverables

The Consultant shall provide the following Services for the Bato Project and any additional Projects undertaken by the Client during the term of this Agreement:

  • Product Requirements Documentation: Preparation of comprehensive Product Requirements Documents (PRDs) including technical specifications, architecture diagrams, database schemas, API endpoint specifications, and smart contract interface specifications for all Projects.
  • Customer Journey Mapping: Development of end-to-end customer journey maps, screen flow architectures, and user experience documentation covering all user interactions from discovery through transaction completion.
  • UI/UX Design Systems: Creation of user interface mockups, mobile screen designs, interactive prototypes, and design system documentation for all user-facing components of the Projects.
  • Executive Presentation Materials: Development of executive-level presentation decks, slideshows, and visual communication materials for stakeholder engagement, investor presentations, and client demonstrations.
  • Technical Architecture: Design and documentation of system architecture including mobile SDK integration, backend services, multi-chain blockchain infrastructure, currency conversion engines, and authentication protocols.
  • Brand and Visual Identity: Development of brand assets, logo designs, colour systems, typography, and visual identity guidelines for the Bato brand and any associated sub-brands.
  • Hardware Product Design: Documentation and design support for hardware components including the PayBand NFC wearable bracelet, including specifications, user flows, and integration architecture.
  • Competitive Analysis: Preparation of competitive positioning documents, market analysis, and strategic recommendations for product-market fit and commercial viability.
  • Any other deliverables reasonably requested by the Client and mutually agreed upon in writing from time to time.
